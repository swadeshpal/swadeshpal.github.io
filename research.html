Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of applied mathematics, computational biology, and complex systems. I utilize mathematical modeling, numerical analysis, and machine learning techniques to understand pattern formation, ecological dynamics, and bioneurological processes.

Mathematical Neuroscience & Neurodegenerative Diseases

A significant portion of my current research involves the mathematical and computational modeling of neurodegenerative disorders, primarily Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases. I investigate the complex dynamics of toxic protein propagation (such as amyloid-beta) and the dual role of astrocytes in disease development. Through multiscale co-simulation and large-scale brain network models, my work aims to capture the nonequilibrium landscapes of calcium ions and proteins to better understand pathology dynamics and evaluate potential anti-amyloid-beta therapies.

Alzheimer's Disease Parkinson's Disease Neural-Glial Dynamics Network Models

Mathematical Biology & Ecological Modeling

Building upon my doctoral thesis on the Effects of Nonlocal Interaction on Spatio-Temporal Pattern Formation, I develop and analyze ecological models such as predator-prey systems. My research explores how spatial and temporal nonlocality, fear effects, and resource consumption influence population distributions. I am also investigating the dynamics of sustainable semiarid ecosystems and analyzing pattern alternations induced by nonlocal intraspecific competitions using reaction-diffusion frameworks.

Reaction-Diffusion Systems Predator-Prey Models Spatio-Temporal Patterns Nonlocal Interactions

Computational Mathematics & Machine Learning

To solve complex nonlinear dynamical systems, I leverage advanced computational techniques. My recent work incorporates Scientific Machine Learning, specifically Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), to detect critical transitions in complex systems and model adaptive anti-tau treatments based on Bayesian approaches. Furthermore, I implement robust numerical solutions for ODEs and PDEs using parallel computing architectures.
